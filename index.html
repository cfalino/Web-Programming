Claudette Colvin


Everyone believes Rosa Parks was the first to protest giving up her seat during the Civil Rights Movement... but was she?

  • On the way home from high school in 1955, then 15-year-old Claudette Colvin refused to get up to make room for a white woman on a city bus in Montgomery, Alabama.

  • According to the rules at that time, if a bus were to become so crowded that all the "white seats" were occupied and a white person was left standing, any African American was expected to get up from his/her seat to allow the white person to sit.

  • The bus driver asked Claudette to get up and Claudette refused. He signaled to police men to come force her to move and she still refused. The policemen forcibly removed her from the bus.
  • This event occurred 9 months prior to Rosa Parks' famous arrest for the exact same offense.

  • Claudette's mother Mary told her daughter "Be quiet about what you did. Let Rosa be the one - white people aren't going to bother Rosa, they like her."
